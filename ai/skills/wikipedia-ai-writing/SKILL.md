# Natural Writing Style Guide

Based on [Wikipedia's Signs of AI Writing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Signs_of_AI_writing).

## Content Patterns to Avoid

### Undue Emphasis on Significance and Legacy
AI tends to puff up importance with statements like:
- "marking a pivotal moment in..."
- "This initiative was part of a broader movement..."
- "highlighting the enduring legacy of..."
- "reflects the continued relevance of..."

**Fix:** State facts plainly without editorializing about their significance. Let readers draw their own conclusions.

### Superficial Analysis with Trailing Clauses
AI attaches vague present-participle phrases to sentences:
- "...emphasizing the importance of solidarity"
- "...demonstrating the ongoing relevance of his research"
- "...underscoring its role as a regional hub"

**Fix:** Delete these trailing clauses. If the significance is real, show it with concrete facts instead.

### Promotional/Advertisement-like Language
AI struggles with neutral tone, especially for "cultural heritage":
- "breathtaking region," "vibrant town," "rich cultural heritage"
- "nestled within," "offers visitors a fascinating glimpse"
- "seamlessly connecting," "dependable, value-driven experiences"

**Fix:** Use neutral, descriptive language. Replace "breathtaking" with specific descriptions. Cut marketing-speak entirely.

### Vague Attributions
AI hedges with weasel words:
- "has been described as..."
- "is of interest to researchers"
- "scholars note that..."
- "several publications have cited"

**Fix:** Either cite a specific source with a specific claim, or remove the attribution.

### "Challenges and Future Prospects" Formula
AI loves ending with:
- "Despite its [positive words], [subject] faces challenges..."
- Followed by vague optimism about "ongoing initiatives"

**Fix:** If challenges exist, integrate them naturally. Don't use this formulaic structure.

## Language and Grammar Patterns to Avoid

### Overused "AI Vocabulary" Words
These words appear far more frequently in AI text than human text:
- **delve, tapestry, pivotal, intricate, multifaceted**
- **nuanced, landscape, realm, testament, underscore**
- **crucial, foster, comprehensive, robust, leverage**
- **facilitate, encompass, paradigm, intricacies**

**Fix:** Use simpler, more common words. "Important" instead of "pivotal." "Complex" instead of "multifaceted."

### Avoidance of "is" and "are"
AI substitutes elaborate constructions:
- "serves as a" instead of "is"
- "features" or "offers" instead of "has"
- "ventured into politics as a candidate" instead of "was a candidate"

**Fix:** Use simple copulas. "The building is a museum" not "The building serves as a museum."

### Rule of Three Overuse
AI constantly uses triple constructions:
- "adjective, adjective, and adjective"
- "professionals, experts, and specialists"
- "keynote sessions, panel discussions, and networking opportunities"

**Fix:** Vary your list lengths. Sometimes two items. Sometimes four. Sometimes none.

### Elegant Variation (Synonym Overuse)
AI avoids repeating words by cycling through synonyms:
- Person's name, then "the protagonist," "the key figure," "the artist"

**Fix:** Repeating a name or term is fine. Use pronouns naturally. Don't strain for synonyms.

### Negative Parallelisms
AI overuses balanced constructions:
- "Not only... but also..."
- "It's not just about X, it's about Y"
- "No... no... just..."

**Fix:** Make your point directly without the rhetorical setup.

### False Ranges
AI uses "from X to Y" constructions that don't form real ranges:
- "from scientific discovery to artistic expression" (no scale)
- "from problem-solving to technological innovation"

**Fix:** Only use ranges when there's an actual continuum or scale.

## Style and Formatting Patterns to Avoid

### Title Case in Headings
AI capitalizes all main words: "The Effects Of Foreign Language Anxiety On Learning"

**Fix:** Use sentence case for most contexts.

### Overuse of Boldface
AI bolds key terms mechanically, often every instance of important words.

**Fix:** Use bold sparingly for genuine emphasis only.

### Inline-Header Vertical Lists
AI loves:
- **Concept:** Description here
- **Implementation:** More description
- **Results:** Even more

**Fix:** Use prose when possible. Lists should be for genuinely listable items.

### Overuse of Em Dashes
AI uses em dashes where commas, parentheses, or colons would be more natural.

**Fix:** Reserve em dashes for genuine interjections. Default to commas.

### Curly Quotes
ChatGPT uses "curly quotes" and apostrophe's where straight ones are standard.

## When Editing for Natural Style

1. **Cut the preamble.** Start with the actual content, not meta-commentary about what you're about to say.

2. **Delete significance claims.** If something is important, the facts will show it.

3. **Use concrete specifics.** Replace "renowned" with actual achievements. Replace "influential" with specific influences.

4. **Vary sentence structure.** Mix short and long. Don't always follow the same pattern.

5. **Read it aloud.** AI text often sounds wrong when spoken because it's optimizing for text patterns, not natural speech.

6. **Check for hedging.** Remove "it's worth noting," "importantly," "significantly," and similar filler.

7. **Simplify vocabulary.** If a simpler word works, use it. "Use" not "utilize." "Help" not "facilitate."

8. **Be direct.** State things once, clearly. Don't rephrase the same idea multiple ways.

## Quick Checklist

Before finalizing, verify your text doesn't:
- [ ] Start with "In today's [X]..." or "In the realm of..."
- [ ] End paragraphs with "...highlighting the importance of..."
- [ ] Use "tapestry," "delve," "multifaceted," "nuanced," or "landscape" metaphorically
- [ ] Have three-item lists in every paragraph
- [ ] Bold multiple terms throughout
- [ ] Use em dashes more than once per paragraph
- [ ] Include vague claims about "researchers" or "scholars" without citations
- [ ] End with a "challenges and future" section
- [ ] Use "serves as" where "is" would work
